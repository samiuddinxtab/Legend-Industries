# Positioning, Claims, and Language Boundaries

## Purpose of This File

This document defines how the business is positioned in language.

Its role is to:
- Lock the meaning of the business in words
- Prevent marketing drift over time
- Constrain AI-generated copy
- Give developers, writers, and future editors a shared truth

This file does not aim to persuade customers.
It aims to **prevent misrepresentation**.

If a line of copy cannot be justified using this document, it must not appear on the website.

---

## Core Positioning Statement (Locked)

**“Manufacturing-backed corporate and bulk utility merchandise solutions for organizations and bulk individual requirements.”**

This sentence is deliberately restrained.

It encodes four truths:
1. The credibility source is manufacturing, not curation.
2. The dominant mode is corporate and organizational buying.
3. The product nature is utility merchandise, not lifestyle gifting.
4. Bulk individual buyers are included without inviting retail behavior.

This sentence must not be shortened, embellished, or replaced with marketing variants.

---

## What This Business IS (In Positioning Terms)

Legend Industries is positioned as:
- A manufacturing-backed supplier
- A customization-capable operation
- A bulk-focused procurement partner
- A provider of functional, brandable utility items

The business solves problems related to:
- Bulk supply
- Custom branding
- Institutional requirements
- Corporate and organizational gifting
- Event and program-based distribution

The positioning emphasizes **capability and reliability**, not novelty.

---

## What This Business Is NOT (Critical Boundaries)

The business is not positioned as:
- A retail brand
- A gift shop
- A lifestyle or premium gifting brand
- A catalog-based seller
- An ecommerce platform
- A “one-stop shop” for everything
- A creative or design-led agency

Any language implying the above is incorrect and must be removed if introduced.

---

## Language Hierarchy (Very Important)

All website language must follow this hierarchy of intent:

1. **Factual**
2. **Operational**
3. **Descriptive**
4. **Only then, lightly confident**

Language must never jump directly to persuasive or emotional tones.

If a sentence sounds like an advertisement, it is wrong by default.

---

## Allowed Claims (Grounded in Reality)

The following types of claims are allowed because they reflect verifiable reality:

- Manufacturing-backed
- Custom branding available
- Bulk quantities handled
- GST-compliant operations
- Institutional and organizational supply
- Context-based pricing
- Procurement-oriented workflow
- Pan-India delivery capability

Claims must be phrased as capabilities, not promises.

Example of acceptable framing:
“This is how we typically work.”
Not:
“This is what we guarantee.”

---

## Forbidden Claims (Non-Negotiable)

The following claims must never appear on the website:

- “Best”, “leading”, “top”, “number one”
- “Premium” (unless legally or contractually defined)
- “Affordable” or “lowest price”
- “Fastest delivery”
- “Guaranteed satisfaction”
- “Trusted by thousands” (unless verifiable and listed)
- “High-quality” without context
- “End-to-end solutions” (too vague)
- “One-stop solution”

These claims create expectations the website cannot enforce and attract the wrong audience.

---

## Vocabulary Control (Subtle but Important)

Certain words are allowed only in specific contexts:

- “Gifting” is allowed only when paired with “corporate”, “institutional”, or “bulk”.
- “Customer” should be avoided; prefer “buyer”, “organization”, or “requirement”.
- “Products” should be used sparingly; prefer “items”, “materials”, or “deliverables”.
- “Order” is acceptable; “purchase” is less preferred.
- “Quote” is acceptable; “price list” is not.

Language choices are a filtering mechanism.

---

## Tone Boundaries

The tone of the site must be:
- Calm
- Neutral
- Confident without assertion
- Clear without oversimplification

The tone must never be:
- Playful
- Trendy
- Emotional
- Inspirational
- Urgent
- Conversational in a sales sense

If the tone feels suitable for a retail homepage, it is incorrect.

---

## Audience Signaling (Implicit, Not Explicit)

The site must signal seriousness through:
- Restraint
- Specificity
- Lack of sales pressure
- Absence of decorative language

The site must not explicitly say “this is only for serious buyers”.
The language itself should perform that filtering.

---

## Consistency Rule (Critical)

Once published:
- Positioning language must remain stable over time.
- New pages must reuse the same framing.
- No seasonal tone shifts.
- No campaign-based language changes.

If business reality changes materially, this file must be updated before the website is.

---

## Final Constraint

Positioning is not a marketing layer.
It is a **truth constraint**.

If future stakeholders want the website to “sound better” or “sell more”, this document must be consulted first.

End of file.
